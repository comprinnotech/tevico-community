Severity Score

The Severity Score indicates your environtment's status based only on the failed checks. The score is calculated in a way where more critical checks contribute more to the score than less critical checks. This allows you to quickly understand the overall infra posture of your environment and prioritize remediation efforts.

Each failed check contributes to the score based on its severity level, while passed checks do not affect the score. The higher the score, the more severe the issues are in your environment.

The weightage of each severity level is as follows:

  • Critical: 4
  • High: 3
  • Medium: 2
  • Low: 1
Total score based on severity is calculated as follows:
  • Critical: 4 * Critical Checks Failed
  • High: 3 * High Checks Failed
  • Medium: 2 * Medium Checks Failed
  • Low: 1 * Low Checks Failed

Severity Score is then calculated based on the following formula:

( (Total score based on severity / Total number of checks executed) / 4) * 100

For example, if you have 10 failed checks executed with 2 critical, 3 high, 2 medium and 3 low severity checks, the calculation would be:

( ( ( (2 * 4) + (3 * 3) + (2 * 2) + (3 * 1) ) / 10) / 4) * 100 = 60

Lower the better!
